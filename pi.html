PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE

Research Experience

  • Postdoctoral Research Fellow, Harvard Medical School, July 2015 – June 2017
    • Physicomechanical cues in stem cell engineering
    • Nanostructured conductive scaffolds for cardiac tissue engineering
    • Actuators for navigation of endovascular catheters
  • Visiting Assistant Professor, Stevens Institute of Technology, August 2013 – August 2014
    • Served as the leader of a group working on the morphology and mechanical characterization of carbon nanomaterials such as carbon nanotubes and carbon nanofibers
  • Graduate Research Assistant, Georgia Institute of Technology, July 2009 – January 2013
    • Investigated the effects of morphology on the mechanical response of uncoated and coated carbon nanotube forests
      • Synthesis: CVD, PECVD, E-beam evaporation, ALD, Photolithography
      • Mechanical testing: Nanoindentation, Tension-compression, Die shear
      • Characterization: SEM, EDX, XPS, Raman spectroscopy, Profilometry
    • Evaluated the effects of synthesis methods (drop-casting and spin-casting) and cross-linking on the quasi-static and visco-elastic properties of polymers.
    • Explored the factors affecting real contact area between rough surfaces and foil-supported carbon nanotube forests by developing FE models to capture the mechanical properties of carbon nanotube forests.
  • Graduate Research Assistant, State University of New York at Buffalo, January 2008 – May 2009
    • Developed FE models in ANSYS and ABAQUS to predict and optimize the themo-mechanical performance of thermal interface materials including micro/nano-pastes, and paste-coated foils.
